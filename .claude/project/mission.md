# IrieFishmongers Project Mission

Version: 1.0

---

# Project Name

IrieFishmongers

---

# Mission Statement

To create Jamaica's leading digital seafood marketplace that connects customers, fish vendors, and delivery providers through a secure, scalable, and efficient platform that makes purchasing fresh seafood simple, transparent, and accessible.

---

# Vision Statement

To become the Caribbean's most trusted seafood commerce ecosystem by enabling local fish vendors to reach more customers, improving seafood distribution efficiency, and supporting regional expansion across CARICOM markets.

---

# Core Business Objective

Provide a single platform where:

- Customers can purchase fresh seafood online.
- Vendors can sell inventory digitally.
- Drivers can manage deliveries efficiently.
- Administrators can oversee platform operations.

The platform must support growth from local marketplace operations to a regional multi-country seafood commerce network.

---

# Target Users

## Customers

Individuals and businesses purchasing seafood.

Examples:

- Households
- Restaurants
- Hotels
- Caterers
- Wholesale buyers

---

## Vendors

Seafood suppliers including:

- Fishermen
- Fish markets
- Seafood distributors
- Seafood wholesalers

---

## Drivers

Independent or contracted delivery personnel responsible for order fulfillment.

---

## Administrators

Platform operators responsible for governance, compliance, analytics, and dispute management.

---

# Platform Principles

All development decisions must prioritize:

1. Freshness
2. Reliability
3. Transparency
4. Security
5. Scalability
6. Customer Experience
7. Vendor Success

No feature should compromise these principles.

---

# Marketplace Principles

The platform is a multi-vendor marketplace.

Requirements:

- Multiple vendors may fulfill a single customer order.
- Inventory is owned by vendors.
- Payments are processed centrally.
- Revenue is distributed automatically.
- Delivery is coordinated by the platform.

---

# Location-Aware Fulfillment

The platform shall optimize order fulfillment based on:

- Customer location
- Vendor proximity
- Product availability
- Delivery efficiency
- Delivery cost

The nearest qualified vendors should be prioritized whenever possible.

---

# Perishable Goods Handling

Seafood is classified as a perishable product.

System design must account for:

- Inventory freshness
- Rapid fulfillment
- Limited cancellation windows
- Non-returnable dispatched products
- Temperature-sensitive logistics

---

# Payment Philosophy

Customers make a single payment.

The platform shall:

- Split payments among vendors
- Deduct platform fees
- Deduct delivery fees
- Record all transactions transparently

All payment calculations must be auditable.

---

# Delivery Philosophy

Delivery operations must:

- Minimize travel distance
- Reduce delivery time
- Reduce transportation costs
- Support multi-vendor pickups

Customer experience must remain seamless regardless of the number of vendors involved.

---

# Scalability Goals

Phase 1

Jamaica Marketplace

Phase 2

National Vendor Network

Phase 3

CARICOM Expansion

The system must support:

- Multi-country operations
- Multi-currency support
- Regional tax rules
- Country-specific logistics

without major architectural redesign.

---

# Technical Objectives

The platform must be:

- Cloud-native
- API-first
- Mobile-ready
- Secure by design
- Highly available
- Horizontally scalable

---

# Success Metrics

The project is successful when:

- Customers can reliably order seafood online.
- Vendors can efficiently manage inventory and sales.
- Orders are fulfilled accurately.
- Deliveries are completed on time.
- Payments are distributed correctly.
- Platform operations scale without degradation.

---

# Claude Code Development Directive

When multiple implementation options exist:

1. Choose the most scalable solution.
2. Choose the most secure solution.
3. Choose the solution that improves vendor efficiency.
4. Choose the solution that improves customer experience.
5. Choose the solution that supports future CARICOM expansion.

Short-term convenience must never compromise long-term platform growth.
